Short list for business ideas.
source
  1. Require little, or no money, avoiding the need for outside investors (i.e you can bootstrap).
  2. Is simple to explain, but hard to conceive. For those of you who have a biology background PCR is the classic example of this.
  3. Is not being worked on by anyone else (i.e. no direct competitors).
  4. Easy for customers to trial and use (i.e. low barriers to selling).
  5. Fits into an existing market so you don't have to create both a new product and a new market at the same time.
  6. Is not dependent of the actions of others (e.g. does not build on Twitter's API).
  7. Has no legal issues --- don't want to spend the next 20 years in litigation with everyone.
  8. Cannot be easily replicated.
  9. The first mover has a major advantage.
  10. Is a natural monopoly that can be defended. Ideas that have a strong network effect are often natural monopolies.
  11. Has a high R0 index (i.e. is viral).
  12. Offers high margins that won't be eroded over time.
  13. Solves an interesting and/or important problem that will make your mother proud (i.e. make a dent in the world).
  14. Provides your customers with such significant value that they will wonder how they once lived without it.
  15. Is sticky --- customers won't switch to other products. Ideas that have a strong network effect are sticky.
  16. Is 100x better than any current product or service.
  17. Can be sold without expensive sales staff (i.e. low touch sales model).
  18. Can be adapted to be used in other markets (i.e. build narrow and expand later).
  19. Generates sufficient cashflow to enable your company to expand as fast as you want without needing external investors.
  20. Doesn't threaten any well-established powers who are able and willing to crush you.
  21. Is of interest to the media (free PR).
  22. Has a narrow target market that can be identified and reached.
  23. Is not dependent on the use of low cost labour.
  24. Can be scaled to support millions of users at a low cost.
  25. Can be sold internationally.
